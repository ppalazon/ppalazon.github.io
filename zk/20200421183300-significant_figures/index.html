What does significant digits mean?

Front

What does significant digits mean?

Back

The number of accurate digits is called the number of significant digits

The counting of significant digits begins from the first nonzero digit from the left and can include digits before the decimal point.

If there is no decimal point, then the final significant digit is underline

What is the number of significant figures in the result of the addition or subtraction?

Front

What is the number of significant figures in the result of the addition or subtraction?

Back

The precision of the sum or difference of two measurements is only as good as the precision of the least precise of the two measurements.

Has no significant figures beyond the last decimal place where both of the original numbers had significant figures. Only the last decimal place of the last significant figure in each of the terms matters

\(1.040 + 0.2134 = 1.253\)

  • \(1.040\) has only 3 significant figures beyond the decimal point
  • \(0.2134\) has 4

The result can not have more than \(3\) significant figures beyond the decimal point

\(1.4 + 2.53 = 3.9\)

Do I have to apply the significant rounding rules to intermediate calculations?

Front

Do I have to apply the significant rounding rules to intermediate calculations?

Back

No, keep as many digits as you can, at least 1 more than implied by the precision of the final result.

Avoid cumulative rounding error
